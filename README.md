# Data-Visualization-Empowering-Business-with-Effective-Insights

## Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the Tata Group Data Visualization Virtual Experience Program on Forage. The objective was to analyze retail transaction data and create meaningful visualizations that help business leaders make data-driven decisions.

The project focuses on answering key business questions raised by the CEO and CMO regarding revenue performance, customer behavior, sales trends, and customer retention.

---

## Business Objectives

### CEO Requirements

* Identify the highest and lowest revenue-generating regions.
* Analyze monthly revenue trends and significant increases/decreases.
* Determine seasonal patterns in sales.
* Identify top customers and their contribution to total revenue.
* Assess whether revenue is concentrated among a few customers or diversified.

### CMO Requirements

* Calculate the percentage of repeat customers.
* Analyze customer reorder frequency.
* Measure revenue generated by repeat customers.
* Identify customers with the highest number of repeat purchases.
* Understand customer retention and loyalty patterns.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains online retail transaction records, including:

* Invoice Number
* Customer ID
* Product Description
* Quantity
* Unit Price
* Invoice Date
* Country

Data cleaning was performed to remove:

* Invalid transactions
* Negative quantities
* Missing customer IDs
* Duplicate records

---

## Key Insights

### Revenue Analysis

* The United Kingdom generated the highest revenue.
* Several smaller regions contributed minimal revenue and present growth opportunities.

### Monthly Sales Trend

* Revenue displayed clear monthly fluctuations.
* Peak sales periods indicated seasonal purchasing behavior.

### Customer Analysis

* Repeat customers contributed the majority of total revenue.
* Most repeat purchases occurred within the first 30 days after a previous purchase.
* A small group of highly loyal customers generated a significant portion of revenue.

### Customer Retention

* Repeat customers accounted for approximately 66% of the customer base.
* Revenue from repeat customers significantly exceeded revenue from one-time buyers.
